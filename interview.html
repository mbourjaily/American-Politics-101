American Democracy Conversation

A non-partisan civic dialogue — who we are, and where we go from here.

Americans are frustrated with polarization and a government that seems not to serve them — but often aren't sure what to do about it. In the words of pollster Frank Luntz, "when a democracy stops learning and a democracy stops listening, that democracy is in trouble." Americans feel "betrayed" because they aren't being listened to — and they want to be heard.

This AI-guided interview exists to hear from you and listen. Its purpose is to understand better what Americans want, with the hope of finding common ground on which to unite us — even as we continue to exercise our freedom to be different, disagree, and contend on the basis of those differences.

What to expect

The conversation takes about 20 minutes, in two halves. The first half explores how you see the current state of American politics — what you understand about how it works, and where you see its strengths and weaknesses. The second half turns to the future — your hopes for the country and your own ideas for addressing the problems you've identified. At the midpoint, you can choose to stop or continue.

There are no right or wrong answers. This isn't a quiz or a partisan poll — your honest perspective is what matters. Responses are saved anonymously for research purposes; no personally identifying information is collected or stored.
